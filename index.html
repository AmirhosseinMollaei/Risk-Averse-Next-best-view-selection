Active Next-Best-View Optimization for Risk-Averse Path Planning

Amirhossein Mollaei Khass, Guangyi Liu, Vivek Pandey, Wen Jiang, Boshu Lei, Kostas Daniilidis, Nader Motee

arXiv preprint, 2025

[PDF on arXiv] [Code] [Video] [BibTeX]

Abstract

Safe navigation in partially known environments requires a robot to reason about both motion risk and the value of future observations. This work introduces a unified framework that starts from a coarse reference path and refines it using risk maps constructed from Average Value-at-Risk (AVaR) statistics computed on an online 3D Gaussian-splat radiance field. The resulting risk field is used to generate locally safe and dynamically feasible trajectories.

In parallel, we formulate next-best-view (NBV) selection as an optimization problem on the pose manifold, where a Riemannian gradient scheme maximizes an expected information gain objective that focuses on regions most critical for imminent motion. By tightly coupling risk-aware path refinement with NBV planning and leveraging decomposable gradients for scalability, the method enables efficient, perception-aware, risk-averse navigation in complex environments.

Method Overview

The framework assumes a robot equipped with onboard sensing and a coarse global reference path. A 3D Gaussian-splatting SLAM backbone maintains an online radiance-field representation of the environment. At every planning cycle, AVaR-based collision risk is evaluated over a grid, yielding a conservative risk field. A local A*-based planner then searches over a filtered safe subset of grid points to synthesize a short horizon, risk-averse trajectory segment toward the next subgoal.

To actively improve the map where it matters most for upcoming motion, the environment is masked around the locally planned trajectory. NBV poses are optimized on SE(3) using a proximity-weighted Fisher-information objective that prioritizes Gaussians inside this masked region. This couples motion planning and active perception: executed controls move the robot along a safe path while the selected views reduce uncertainty in high-risk zones.

Results

We validate the proposed approach in simulated environments by comparing the refined, risk-averse trajectories against shortest-path baselines. The risk-aware paths maintain greater clearance from high-risk regions at the cost of modest path-length increases, and the NBV strategy yields more informative reconstructions near the executed trajectories. Quantitative evaluations highlight improvements in tail-risk metrics and map quality relative to existing baselines.
